About Me

Hi! My name is Sarah, and I also go by the nickname SJ. I grew up in Lower Merion, a suburb outside of Philadelphia, in a working-class family. I recently graduated from the University of Pittsburgh Dietrich School of Arts and Sciences with a Bachelor of Science in Environmental Science and a Bachelor of Arts in Public and Professional Writing, as well as a minor in Gender, Sexuality, and Women’s Studies and a certificate in Public Communication of Science and Technology. I also graduated with a Joint Honors Degree from the University of Pittsburgh Frederick Honors College.

In my free time, I like to read, travel, listen to podcasts, go hiking, do yoga, and spend time with my pets. For the past year, I have also worked as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for two courses: Writing for the Sciences and Communication for Environmental Professionals. For the past two years, I have also worked as a Senior Staff Writer for the Opinions Desk of The Pitt News. My personal values are grounded in environmentalism, social justice, and animal rights, and my interests lie in environmental advocacy, journalism, and other professional communications.

In the future, I hope to do advocacy work in the field of environmental communications. After my experience this past summer interning with an advocacy group called the Factory Farming Awareness Coalition, combined with my experiences working as a journalist for The Pitt News and an undergraduate teaching assistant for two writing courses, I would like to write articles, edit the work of others, or oversee communications for a nonprofit group relating to the environmental sector. Some fields of interest include factory farming, climate change policy, and sustainable urban planning. These areas connect to my personal values by sustaining my love for animals, respect for the environment, and interest in urban sustainability.
